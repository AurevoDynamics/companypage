Hybrid Zinc-Ion Energy Systems Without Lithium

Battery-level energy. Capacitor-level power. Architecturally controlled safety.

What We Are Building

Aurevo Dynamics is developing Hybrid Zinc-Ion Capacitors (HZIC) that combine aqueous zinc battery chemistry with electronically controlled capacitor buffering. Our architecture is designed to deliver high transient power while enforcing electrochemically safe zinc operation. By integrating solvation-engineered gel electrolytes and active DC–DC charge control, we aim to enable safer, scalable alternatives to lithium-based systems.

The Problem

Lithium-ion systems dominate energy storage, but they face material volatility, thermal runaway risks, and high-cost scaling challenges. Zinc systems are safer and abundant — but suffer from dendrites, poor high-rate stability, and limited cycle life.

Existing architectures do not adequately decouple transient power demand from zinc electrochemistry.

Our Technology

Aurevo Dynamics develops Hybrid Zinc-Ion Capacitors (HZICs) that merge battery-grade energy storage with capacitor-grade power delivery. By combining zinc metal anodes, MnO₂-based cathodes, and EDLC buffering under active electronic control, our architecture decouples high-power transients from zinc electrochemistry.

Core Innovation

Solvation-Regulated Gel Electrolytes

A polymer-engineered gel electrolyte that modulates Zn²⁺ solvation, reduces dendrite formation, and improves cycle stability under controlled current densities.

Active EDLC–Battery Decoupling

A DC–DC CC–CV power stage electronically enforces zinc-safe operating limits while routing transient loads through EDLC buffering.

System-Level Safety

Multi-layer electrochemical, electronic, and thermal constraints ensure intrinsic safety without reliance on extreme electrolytes or rigid physical barriers.

System Architecture

The HZIC platform integrates zinc cells, EDLC modules, and intelligent power electronics into a modular, scalable system. Current partitioning is enforced electronically rather than passively, enabling fast charging and high-power delivery without zinc degradation.

Development Stage

Aurevo Dynamics is currently in the pre-prototype engineering phase. Core system architecture, electrolyte formulation strategy, and charge-control electronics design have been completed at the documentation and modelling level.
